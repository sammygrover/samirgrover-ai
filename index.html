bio

I'm a gastroenterologist and medical educator at the University of Toronto and Scarborough Health Network. I spent 14 years at St. Michael's Hospital doing endoscopy, teaching residents, running simulation research, and eventually directing the GI training program. In 2023 I moved to SHN to help stand up the clinical teaching infrastructure for SAMIH — UofT's newest medical academy. I've published 120+ papers, mostly on how people learn to do procedures and what happens when they don't. More recently I've been thinking about what happens to all of that when AI enters the picture.

thesis

I've spent my career studying how physicians develop competence — in endoscopy, in clinical reasoning, in the messy human process of learning to be a doctor. The question I keep coming back to is: what happens to that process when machines start getting genuinely good at the things we've been training humans to do?

Not in the distant future. Now. AI can already detect polyps better than most endoscopists. It can read imaging, draft notes, synthesize evidence. The diagnostic and procedural tasks we've built entire training programs around are becoming automatable. That changes everything — not just what we teach, but why, and what it means to be a physician at all.

I think we need to redesign medical education from the ground up — not to compete with AI, but to prepare physicians for a world where they work alongside it. That means teaching judgment over memorization, building over consuming, and learning to be the kind of doctor a machine can't be. That's what I'm working on.

teaching & programs
SAMIH — Scarborough Academy of Medicine and Integrated Health
I lead SHN and its clinical affiliates as teaching sites for UofT's 5th undergraduate medical academy. The new Myron & Berna Garron Health Sciences Complex ($130M) opens fall 2026 at UTSC. MD program launches 2027, with NP, PA, and PT programs in 2026.
The AI-Augmented Gastroenterologist
A clinician-developer elective I co-designed for GI fellows. Three 90-minute sessions: vibe-code a clinical prototype, red-team it with faculty, demo it to leadership. Funded by UofT GI Division Innovation Grant. Co-led with Dr. Joshua Landy. Launching October 2026.
Summer of Vibes — SHN RISE Program
A student-driven healthcare innovation sprint I founded at SHN. Undergraduates build working AI prototypes for real clinical problems over 10–16 weeks. 2025 pilot: 13 projects. 2026: ~50 students across 8 innovation taskforces. Grand Demo Day at UTSC.
UofT Endoscopic Simulation Course
I founded and have directed this course for 16 years. Focus on deliberate practice, competency assessment, and cognitive load in procedural training.
