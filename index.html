bio

I'm a gastroenterologist, medical educator, and education researcher at the University of Toronto and Scarborough Health Network. As EVP Academics at SHN, I lead the network and its clinical affiliates as teaching sites for SAMIH — the University of Toronto's newest medical academy. My current work focuses on how artificial intelligence is reshaping clinical training, professional identity, and what it means to be a physician. I have over 120 peer-reviewed publications and hold the Pialis Family Chair in Education Research.

the thesis

AI systems are approaching — and in some domains have already reached — the diagnostic and procedural capability of trained physicians. I call this threshold the Karpathy Endpoint. It's not a question of if but when and how fast.

The implications go far beyond job displacement. If AI can do the cognitive and perceptual work we've trained doctors to do for centuries, then medical education, professional identity, and health systems all need to be fundamentally redesigned. We need to stop training physicians as knowledge repositories and start training them as orchestrators, ethical decision-makers, and builders.

That's what I'm working on — in writing, in research, and by helping build a medical school that takes this seriously from day one.

teaching & programs
SAMIH — Scarborough Academy of Medicine and Integrated Health
I lead SHN and its clinical affiliates as teaching sites for UofT's 5th undergraduate medical academy. The new Myron & Berna Garron Health Sciences Complex ($130M) opens fall 2026 at UTSC. MD program launches 2027, with NP, PA, and PT programs in 2026.
The AI-Augmented Gastroenterologist
A clinician-developer elective I co-designed for GI fellows. Three 90-minute sessions: vibe-code a clinical prototype, red-team it with faculty, demo it to leadership. Funded by UofT GI Division Innovation Grant. Co-led with Dr. Joshua Landy. Launching October 2026.
Summer of Vibes — SHN RISE Program
A student-driven healthcare innovation sprint I founded at SHN. Undergraduates build working AI prototypes for real clinical problems over 10–16 weeks. 2025 pilot: 13 projects. 2026: ~50 students across 8 innovation taskforces. Grand Demo Day at UTSC.
UofT Endoscopic Simulation Course
I founded and have directed this course for 16 years. Focus on deliberate practice, competency assessment, and cognitive load in procedural training.
