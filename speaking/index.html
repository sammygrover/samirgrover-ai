about

I speak to medical schools, health systems, academic departments, and industry audiences about what AI actually changes in medicine and medical education. The talks draw on 120+ peer-reviewed publications, two decades of teaching physicians procedures, and the programs I run now: a research lab focused on AI in medical education (Grover Lab), the Summer of Vibes innovation sprint, and the academic portfolio of a large Canadian hospital network. Formats: keynotes, grand rounds, workshops, panels, and executive briefings. Virtual or in person, based in Toronto.

signature talks
Neverskilling, deskilling, hyperskilling: training doctors in the age of AI
AI can perform cognitive tasks FOR trainees instead of WITH them, and skills never acquired cannot be maintained. This talk lays out the evidence on skill acquisition and decay in the AI era, and the training designs that produce hyperskilled clinicians instead. For medical educators, program directors, and CBME leaders.
Build, don't just use: the clinician-developer
Clinicians who can build AI tools, not just consume them, are the scarcest resource in health care. The story of how we taught students and physicians to ship working clinical prototypes in weeks, told through the Summer of Vibes program and the clinician-developer electives that followed. For innovation teams, hospital executives, and health-professions faculties.

Talks are tailored to the audience. Related topics: simulation-based training, competency assessment, cognitive load in procedural medicine, conflicts of interest in clinical guidelines.
